WE CAN BEGIN THE WORK OF ADDRESSING THESE BARRIERS BY IDENTIFYING THE KIND OF ALLYSHIP WE’RE COMFORTABLE ENGAGING IN.

WE MAY NEED TO CHALLENGE
OURSELVES TO AVOID BEING:

A CHAMPION

Someone who vocally supports individuals from marginalized groups to make sure their value is recognized.

AN AMPLIFIER

Someone who makes sure underrepresented voices are heard, respected, and included.

AN ADVOCATE

Someone who uses their power to invite diversity into professional spaces that aren’t as diverse as they could be.

A SPONSOR

Someone who highlights the strengths, accomplishments, and potential of people from marginalized groups.

A CONFIDANT

Someone who creates safe spaces for people by listening to their fears, frustrations, and by being supportive.

AN UPSTANDER

Someone who interrupts and pushes back against inappropriate jokes or comments and protests unjust policies or procedures

A SCHOLAR

Someone who takes the time to learn about the challenges and prejudices people from different marginalized groups experiences by doing their own.
